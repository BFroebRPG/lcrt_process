This site provides an overview of the land use forecasting process used to develop estimates of jobs, residents, and building floor area in the Low Country Rapid Transit (LCRT) corridor from the Downtown Charleston peninsula to Summerville.

The objective of the forecasting process is to update 2040 socioeconomic estimates that inform regional travel models and assessments the ridership potential of BRT.

To account for the influence of transit stations on local development potential, the forecasting process focuses on individual parcels and results are summarized to the travel analysis zones (TAZs) used in ridership modeling. Both parcels and TAZs are orgnized into seven major segments, each representing distinct development markets in the LCRT corridor.

The forecasting process is organized into three key phases:

  • Known development: How much development is currently on the ground? Where is development activity ongoing?

  • Allocation preparation: How much new development is reasonable to expect by 2040? What sites are suitable for development? What kind and amount of development is appropriate at each site?

  • Allocation results: Where is growth through 2040 expected to go, assuming BRT station locations and typologies?
